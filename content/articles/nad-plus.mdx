## Chemical Background

NAD+ has the chemical formula C21H27N7O14P2 with a molar mass of 663.43 g/mol. The CAS registry number is 53-84-9. The compound is also known by the following synonyms: Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide, NAD, Coenzyme I, DPN. Additional chemical information can be found in PubChem (ID: 5892). The structural properties of NAD+ contribute to its biological activity and research applications.

NAD+ consists of two nucleotides joined through their phosphate groups. One nucleotide contains an adenine base and the other contains nicotinamide. The molecule exists in two forms: NAD+ (oxidized) and NADH (reduced), which interconvert during metabolic reactions. This redox cycling is fundamental to cellular energy metabolism and makes NAD+ a critical component of numerous biochemical pathways.

The structural characteristics of NAD+ allow it to function as a coenzyme in a wide variety of enzymatic reactions. Its ability to accept and donate electrons makes it essential for energy transfer processes in cells, including those occurring in glycolysis, the citric acid cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation.
