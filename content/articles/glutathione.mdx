## Chemical Background

Glutathione has the chemical formula C10H17N3O6S with a molar mass of 307.32 g/mol. The CAS registry number is 70-18-8. The compound is also known by the following synonyms: GSH, L-Glutathione, Reduced glutathione, γ-L-Glutamyl-L-cysteinylglycine. Additional chemical information can be found in PubChem (ID: 124886). The structural properties of Glutathione contribute to its biological activity and research applications.

Glutathione consists of three amino acids: glutamic acid, cysteine, and glycine, linked by peptide bonds. The unique structure includes a γ-peptide bond between glutamic acid and cysteine (rather than the typical α-peptide bond), which protects the compound from degradation by standard peptidases. The cysteine residue contains a thiol (-SH) group that is critical for the compound's antioxidant and redox functions.

The molecule exists in two forms: reduced glutathione (GSH) and oxidized glutathione (GSSG), which form a redox couple. The GSH/GSSG ratio is an important indicator of cellular redox state and oxidative stress levels. In healthy cells, GSH typically accounts for more than 90% of total glutathione, maintaining a high GSH/GSSG ratio that supports cellular antioxidant capacity.

## Handling and Storage Guidelines

Storage Requirements:
Store lyophilized powder at -20°C in a dry environment. Protect from light, air, and moisture. Reconstituted solution should be stored at 4°C and used promptly. Maintain sterile conditions during reconstitution.

Handling Guidelines:
Handle using sterile techniques and appropriate laboratory personal protective equipment (PPE). Use in well-ventilated areas. Avoid inhalation, ingestion, or skin contact. Glutathione is sensitive to oxidation and should be protected from air exposure. Follow standard laboratory safety protocols. Refer to Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) for complete safety information.

Shelf Life:
24 months when stored properly as lyophilized powder under recommended conditions (-20°C, dry environment, protected from light, air, and moisture).

Reconstitution Notes:
When reconstituting Glutathione, use sterile water or appropriate buffer solutions. Maintain sterile conditions throughout the reconstitution process. Store reconstituted solutions at 4°C and use promptly to minimize oxidation. Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles, as this may affect compound stability and biological activity. Protect reconstituted solutions from air exposure by using airtight containers or nitrogen gas overlay.

Oxidation Sensitivity:
Glutathione is particularly sensitive to oxidation and should be protected from air exposure during storage, handling, and experimental use. The reduced form (GSH) can oxidize to glutathione disulfide (GSSG) when exposed to oxygen. Use airtight containers, minimize exposure time to air, and consider using nitrogen or argon gas to displace oxygen in storage containers. Monitor the GSH/GSSG ratio in experimental samples to ensure the compound remains in its active reduced form.
